STEM MASTERING, ABLETON SHOW PROGRAMMING & MUSICAL SYSTEMS DESIGN

A live show should feel bigger than the record — not like the record has been dismantled.

This service combines stem mastering, Ableton show programming, and musical systems design to help artists bring produced records to the stage without losing the punch, movement, and emotional weight of the original release.

Using a Pro Tools-based stem mastering process, I reconstruct the behavior of the mastered mix and export playback stems that retain far more of the record's energy and cohesion than a standard stem bounce.


Why Show Stems Often Don't Hit Like the Record

A typical stem export breaks the record apart.

On the master, the impact often depends on interactions between parts: compression, limiting, saturation, automation, and the internal push-pull between drums, bass, synths, effects, and vocals. Once those elements are separated into playback stems, many of those interactions disappear.

The result is familiar: the drums feel smaller, the low end gets cloudier, the vocal sits on top instead of inside the track, and the show loses some of the size and authority that made the production exciting in the first place.


How the Stem Mastering Process Works

Most playback stems lose impact because the dynamics of the finished record are no longer being driven by the full mix. Once the master is broken apart into separate stem groups, the compression, limiting, and internal dynamic relationships that made the record hit are no longer reacting to the full arrangement.

My process is designed to preserve that mastered behavior across the exported stems. I load the original studio master and the full stem set into Pro Tools, then rebuild the feel of the released master on the summed stems by ear using compression and limiting until the stem mix responds like the record.

Once that behavior is matched, I print the reconstructed full mix and use it as a sidechain control signal for the mastering dynamics. Each stem is then solo-bounced through a chain that is reacting to that same printed mix, so the exported stems retain more of the movement, punch, and cohesion of the finished production.

The result is a playback-ready stem set that feels closer to the record under live conditions, while still leaving the flexibility needed for lead vocal, live players, click, cues, and monitor mixes.

Musical Systems Design

This is not only a technical prep service. It is also a show design service.

Musical systems design means looking at the artist's set, arrangements, live personnel, monitoring needs, and performance goals, then building a playback and routing strategy that makes the show feel more intentional, more cinematic, and easier to perform.

Sometimes that means tightening transitions, building cue structures, and organizing sessions so the performer can stay fully in the moment. Sometimes it means using playback, automation, and routing to create moments that feel larger, cleaner, or more emotionally focused than they would otherwise.

The point is not technology for its own sake. The point is a better show.


Who This Is For

  • Artists moving from studio release to live performance
  • Bands adding playback for the first time
  • Acts whose current stems feel flat, crowded, or underpowered on stage
  • Artists who want a more premium and intentional live presentation
  • Teams that need both technical execution and musical judgment

Workflow

Projects typically begin with review of the existing tracks, stems, and live goals. From there, the playback architecture is designed around the act: what needs to feel huge, what needs to stay flexible, what the vocalist needs in order to perform confidently, and how the technical system should support the emotional arc of the set.

Deliverables can range from refined stem sets and a clean Ableton session to a more complete show package including routing, automation, cueing, and performance support.
