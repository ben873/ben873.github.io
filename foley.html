FOLEY

Foley is the human layer of sound — footsteps, cloth movement, and physical interaction that connects performance to picture. When done well, it’s rarely noticed, but its absence is immediately felt.

Foley editing encompasses the full Foley workflow, from spotting and recording through editorial integration, ensuring these details feel intentional, natural, and grounded in the final mix.


What Foley Includes

Foley editing is not limited to cutting footsteps into a timeline. The service typically includes:

  • Spotting – identifying moments where Foley best supports performance and clarity
  • Recording – capturing footsteps, cloth, and prop movement appropriate to picture and character
  • Editorial Integration – syncing, shaping, and blending Foley so it sits naturally within the soundtrack

The goal is always realism and restraint — enhancing physical presence without distracting from dialogue, music, or story.


How Foley Fits Into Audio Post Production

Foley editing works in close coordination with dialogue editorial, sound design, and the final re-recording mix. It reinforces performance detail while leaving space for dialogue intelligibility and musical dynamics.

Integrated early, Foley provides flexibility in the mix and reduces the need for heavy processing later in the pipeline.

Learn more about the broader workflow on the Audio Post Production page.


When a Project Benefits From Foley

  • Scenes with significant movement or close-up performance
  • Noisy or compromised production sound
  • Intimate moments where physical detail supports emotion
  • Projects aiming for a polished, theatrical-quality soundtrack
